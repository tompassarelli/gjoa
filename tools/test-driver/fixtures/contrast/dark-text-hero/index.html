A morning on the coast, told in plain dark ink

The text in this hero was authored dark on purpose, because the photo behind it is light and airy. This is the case a naive dark-mode fix gets wrong: if the engine assumes every image is dark and forces the text light, the words vanish into the pale backdrop. The correct behavior is to leave this dark text alone.

Below the hero the page continues with ordinary body copy on a white background. That part inverts cleanly like any other light page. The interesting region is the hero above, where the engine must look at the actual composited backdrop rather than guessing, and recognize that dark text on a light image is already perfectly legible and should be preserved exactly as written.

Getting this wrong is the regression that prompted the third revision of the dark-mode resolver. An earlier version flipped all light-image text to white for safety, which silently destroyed every hero that had been designed the other way around. This fixture exists so that mistake can never ship unnoticed again.
